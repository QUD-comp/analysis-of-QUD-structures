What is the way things are?
>Should the morning-after pill be sold in pharmacies?
>>Being a pharmacist myself, I argue categorically in favour of over-the-counter availability of the morning-after pill in pharmacies.
>>Why should the morning-after pill be available in pharmacies?
>>>From a medical perspective the morning-after pill works best when taken as soon as possible after intercourse.
>>>Why do pharmacies help with the quick availability of the morning-after pill?
>>>>Always having to visit the doctor first (including travel and waiting time, possible embarrassment, etc.) leads to more unwanted pregnancies.
>>>What follows when the morning-after pill is available in pharmacies?
>>>>Many opponents of the pill point out that this does away with the consultation at the doctor's and many women take it unnecessarily.
>>>Are pharmacists qualified enough to sell the morning-after pill?
>>>>However, the fact is that we pharmacists are legally required to inform the patients and possess the same subject matter expertise as doctors.
